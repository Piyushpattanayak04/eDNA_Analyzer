The Discovery Bottleneck

Traditional methods of species identification are slow, expensive, and often require physical specimens. Environmental DNA (eDNA) offers a powerful alternative, but its analysis is plagued by incomplete reference databases and classifiers that cannot identify what they haven't seen before. This "closed-world" assumption is the single biggest barrier to discovering the true scope of biodiversity.

The PhyloDive Revolution

PhyloDive shatters this limitation. By combining deep learning with anomaly detection, our platform not only classifies known organisms with state-of-the-art accuracy but also flags and clusters potential new species, opening a new frontier in biodiversity research.

The Challenge in Genomic Classification

Current methods for eDNA analysis face critical limitations that stifle discovery.

Database Gaps & Errors

Reference databases suffer from mislabeled entries, taxonomic conflicts, and vast coverage gaps, leading to inaccurate or incomplete classifications.

  • Mislabeled sequences
  • Incomplete taxonomic trees
  • Poor representation of rare taxa

The "Closed-World" Problem

Traditional classifiers can only assign a sequence to a known category. Novel organisms are either misclassified or discarded, rendering them invisible.

  • High-confidence misclassifications
  • Novelty is treated as error
  • Discovery potential is lost

Algorithmic Limitations

Simple models fail to learn the complex, hierarchical patterns in genomic data, leading to poor generalization and limited error tolerance.

  • Lack of contextual understanding
  • Sensitivity to sequencing errors
  • Inability to model taxonomy

Our Solution: A Multi-Stage AI Engine

PhyloDive's hybrid architecture for comprehensive taxonomic analysis.

Hybrid AI Engine

PhyloDive employs a sophisticated three-stage AI architecture that goes beyond traditional classification, enabling both accurate identification and novel discovery.

1

Hierarchical Classification

A multi-task Transformer predicts the full taxonomic lineage, respecting biological hierarchy.

2

Novelty Detection

An autoencoder identifies sequences that are anomalous to the known genetic landscape.

3

Discovery Clustering

Anomalous sequences are grouped into potential new taxa for further investigation.

End-to-End Analysis Pipeline

From raw sequences to actionable insights and novel discoveries.

1. Data Preprocessing

QIIME 2 & DADA2 pipelines generate high-fidelity Amplicon Sequence Variants (ASVs).

2. Sequence Encoding

Advanced k-mer embeddings create rich, contextual representations of sequences.

3. AI Analysis

Our multi-stage AI engine classifies known taxa and detects novel organisms.

4. Visualization & Discovery

Results are explored through interactive trees, maps, and a discovery workbench.
